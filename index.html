Introduction

We introduce Goedel-Prover-V2, an open-source model series setting a new state-of-the-art in automated theorem proving. By building on a standard expert iteration and reinforcement learning pipeline, we introduce three key innovations: (1) scaffolding data synthesis to bridge the gap between simple and complex proofs, (2) compiler-guided self-correction to mimic human-like iterative refinement, and (3) model averaging to maintain diversity.

Our 8B model matches the performance of models nearly 100x its size, and our 32B model significantly outperforms all prior open-source and closed-source provers, establishing a new benchmark for what's possible in automated reasoning.

Our Methodology

A cycle of data synthesis, iterative training, and novel refinement techniques.

Scaffolding Data Synthesis

To overcome data scarcity, we automatically generate intermediate problems. This creates a smoother difficulty curve, bridging the gap from simple lemmas to complex theorems and providing a denser training signal.

Compiler-Guided Self-Correction

Inspired by human problem-solving, we train the model to use feedback from the Lean compiler to iteratively correct its own proofs, integrating this task into both supervised fine-tuning and reinforcement learning.

Model Averaging

To combat overfitting and loss of diversity in later training stages, we average trained checkpoints with the base model. This simple technique restores diversity and boosts Pass@K performance for larger K.

Expert Iteration & RL

We follow a proven pipeline: formalizing problems, generating and verifying proofs, and using the new, successful proofs to train the next generation of models, further enhanced by reinforcement learning.
